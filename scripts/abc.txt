            <h1 id="pot" class="display-4" style="margin-left: 15px;">Powertrain</h1>



            <p class="lead" style="text-align: justify; margin: 0 0 0 15px;">
                Powertrain system is essentially the heart of the vehicle starting from the engine
                and ending at the wheels. The department essentially deals with the
                transmission of power taking power from the motor and delivering it to the
                wheels with the help of gears , shafts etc.<br>
                The department employs an extensive research model about the sub-systems
                namely intake, exhaust, cooling (radiator) and fuel tank so that the sub-systems
                work in perfect synergy in order to produce high levels of efficiency and
                drivability. The department focuses on tuning of the engine, along with
                designing and manufacturing of highly tuned intake and exhaust manifolds for
                increased output in mid RPM range, Programmable ECU and customized
                cooling to ensure optimal running of the engine.
            </p>

                <h1 id="vd" class="display-4" style="margin-right: 15px;">Vehicle Dynamics</h1>
                <p class="lead" style="text-align: justify;  margin: 0 15px 0 0;">

                    Vehicle dynamics deals with the suspension geometry along with steering
                    geometry and the wheel assembly of the car .The primary objective of the
                    department is to design and develop a highly tuneable and robust system and
                    track tune it. It also involves developing a geometry which maximizes the grip
                    at peak cornering. The secondary objective is to achieve a tremendous reduction
                    in un-sprung mass without compromising structural integrity. The department
                    also aims to analyse the behaviour of the individual subsystems in static
                    condition, then compile it for the vehicle and compare it with dynamic
                    behaviour of the vehicle. Telemetry system is employed while testing to acquire
                    data from the car and driver feedback is factored in to set up the car in the best
                    possible way for various tracks.
                </p>
            </div>
