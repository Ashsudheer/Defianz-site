TEAM STRUCTURE

subdivisions in the team

Departments

Powertrain

Powertrain system is essentially the heart of the vehicle starting from the engine and ending at the wheels. The department essentially deals with the transmission of power taking power from the motor and delivering it to the wheels with the help of gears , shafts etc.
The department employs an extensive research model about the sub-systems namely intake, exhaust, cooling (radiator) and fuel tank so that the sub-systems work in perfect synergy in order to produce high levels of efficiency and drivability. The department focuses on tuning of the engine, along with designing and manufacturing of highly tuned intake and exhaust manifolds for increased output in mid RPM range, Programmable ECU and customized cooling to ensure optimal running of the engine.

Vehicle Dynamics

Vehicle dynamics deals with the suspension geometry along with steering geometry and the wheel assembly of the car .The primary objective of the department is to design and develop a highly tuneable and robust system and track tune it. It also involves developing a geometry which maximizes the grip at peak cornering. The secondary objective is to achieve a tremendous reduction in un-sprung mass without compromising structural integrity. The department also aims to analyse the behaviour of the individual subsystems in static condition, then compile it for the vehicle and compare it with dynamic behaviour of the vehicle. Telemetry system is employed while testing to acquire data from the car and driver feedback is factored in to set up the car in the best possible way for various tracks.

Aerodynamics

The designing of the aerodynamics is focused primarily reducing lap times by maximizing downforce while minimizing the associated drag penalty and centred around reliability, low weight, manufacturing feasibility while ensuring appreciable aesthetics. The department deals with designing of a front wing , rear wing , diffuser, nose and sidepods alongside of the car.

Chassis

The objective of the department is to provide a design in order to make the lightest possible rule-compliant chassis while maximizing torsional rigidity and achieving a compact yet ergonomic structure. Every race car competition has an extensive set of stringent rules, pertaining to the design, to provide the necessary safety and to prevent the competition from becoming unfair. The department aims to design a lightweight hybrid race car chassis consisting of a fibre reinforced composite monocoque combining structural, aesthetic, ergonomic and crash properties, and a tubular space frame engine compartment, meeting stiffness and strength demands while remaining easy to maintain and manufacture, thus keeping production cost low.

Electronics & Software

The primary objective of the department is to facilitate the fine-tuning of the car’s setup by providing real-time track data and develop an interactive driver modelling and training platform. In addition to the aforementioned, the electronics branch also aims to design and fabricate a reliable electrical power management system for the vehicle. It is responsible for ensuring that proper current is supplied to all the sensors. Working in tandem is the software branch which deals with data actuation. The software teams handles the log of the data which enables it for future reference and further testing. The department also focuses on one of the most important elements of the project, telemetry.

Drive Train

The prime focus was to minimize weight, optimize final drive ratio and torque bias. The Drexler FSAE Salisbury clutch pack differential weighs less and performs better compared to the previous Torsen Differential. shafts were introduced minimizing rotational inertia. A chain tensioning mechanism using stainless steel spacers lead to a simpler design and weight reduction. An electronic gear shifter actuated via steering mounted buttons ensures better ergonomics, faster shifting hence improved lap times.

Management

It consist of three departments:

Corporate : department focus on geeting financial support and in kind sponsors throughout the season.

Administration : department maintains communication bridge between university and team . Department focus on making travel and meeting arrangements, preparing reports and maintaining appropriate filing systems to fulfill the necessary requirements of the team .

Finance and Logistics : this department deals with day to day transactions , keeping the account of the team’s finances. Reimbursement of the bills for the various parts and tools . The logistics deals in making arrangements for the amenities required in the workshop .
